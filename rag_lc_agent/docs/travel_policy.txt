# GlobalCorp Internal Policy: Business Travel and Reimbursement
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

0. SUMMARY
This policy outlines the guidelines for business travel, reimbursement, and remote work expectations for all GlobalCorp employees.

1. TRAVEL APPROVAL
All business travel must be approved by the Department Head at least three weeks before the departure date. International travel requires additional approval from the CFO.

2. FLIGHTS AND TRANSPORTATION
- Employees must book economy class for all flights under 6 hours.
- Premium Economy is allowed for international flights exceeding 6 hours.
- Business Class is only permitted for VP-level and above, or with explicit CFO approval for medical reasons.
- Taxis, Ubers, and Lyfts are reimbursable for transport to/from airports and hotels.

3. ACCOMMODATIONS
- Employees should stay in "Preferred Hotels" listed in the Travel Portal.
- The maximum nightly rate for domestic cities is $250 (excluding NYC, SF, and DC, which allow up to $400).
- International rates are subject to the "Global Lodging Index" published on the intranet.

4. MEALS AND INCIDENTALS (PER DIEM)
GlobalCorp provides a daily per diem based on the destination:
- Standard Domestic: $75/day.
- High-Cost Cities (NYC, SF, London, Tokyo, Zurich): $120/day.
- Other International: $90/day.
Note: If a meal is provided by a host or as part of a conference, the per diem must be reduced by $20 (breakfast), $25 (lunch), or $40 (dinner).

5. REIMBURSEMENT PROCESS
- Expense reports must be submitted through Concur within 30 days of returning from travel.
- Receipts are required for all individual expenses exceeding $25.
- Personal expenses (minibar, laundry for trips under 4 days, spa services) are NOT reimbursable.
- The "Travel Reimbursement Hotline" for issues is 1-888-GLOBAL-TRV.
